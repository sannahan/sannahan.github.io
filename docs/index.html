About the Property Project

If you are planning on investing in property in Finland, you should read this blog post! We have collected data on property values, rental rates and population trends to help you identify areas for profitable property investments.

Rental rates have been steadily increasing across Finland. Changes in property value tend to correlate with changes in population.

Below, we have collected relevant data on the 20 biggest cities in Finland: Espoo-Kauniainen, Helsinki, Hämeenlinna, Joensuu, Jyväskylä, Kotka, Kouvola, Kuopio, Lahti, Lappeenranta, Mikkeli, Oulu, Pori, Porvoo, Rovaniemi, Seinäjoki, Tampere, Turku, Vaasa and Vantaa. For all cities, you can see the total population, sales price per square meter and rent per square meter for one-room, two-room and three-room properties between 2011 and 2022. We have gathered this data from the reliable StatFin database and combined it into a simple graph to help you start your property investment journey!

We have two exclusive tips for you when it comes to examining the data above!

  • First, rents have steadily increased in all 20 cities. This is good news for those property investors who rent out their properties!
  • Second, population growth (or lack thereof) seems to correlate with property value. All areas with a declining population (Kotka, Kouvola, Mikkeli and Pori) have seen declines in property values. Areas with rapid population growth of over 10 000 residents between 2011-2022 (Espoo-Kauniainen, Helsinki, Jyväskylä, Kuopio, Oulu, Tampere, Turku and Vantaa) have only seen considerable, steady increases in property value.

Now let's examine the data between the cities. Below you can see comparisons in terms of sales & rent prices. It's easy to see that rent prices in the capital area have been significantly greater and have been increasing more rapidly than in other cities. As for sold apartments, square meter prices for Vantaa are left behind Helsinki & Espoo-Kauniainen and are more in line with other major cities, Turku and Tampere. Looking at other cities, we can see that the rental prices have been increasing in all cities and apartments sizes. For sold apartments, apart from the biggest cities the prices have been more steady or even decreasing.

When thinking of investment possibilites, one would of course like to know what sort of profit can be expected for the investment made. That's why below we're comparing rent prices against the sales prices. Toggle the year filter to see how prices have evolved over the years. It can be seen that one-room apartments offer greater rent profit, allthough it must be noted that they also tend to have higher sales prices per square meter when compared to the larger apartments.

If we divide rent per month / m2 by sales price / m2, we can compare the rent profit between the cities. The graph below shows that Kotka & Kouvola stand out from the rest of the cities, having the best rent / sales price ratio in all apartment sizes. It turns out that the biggest cities having the highest sales prices have the lowest rent / sales price ratios. This means that the rent profit is not as good as in the smaller cities. Helsinki being the most expensive in rents & sales prices has clearly the poorest profit ratio. In 2022 the ratio in Helsinki was nearly three times smaller than in Kotka & Kouvola within all apartmens sizes.
